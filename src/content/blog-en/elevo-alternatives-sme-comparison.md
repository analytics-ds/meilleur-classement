## Why look for an Elevo alternative?

Elevo has a clear positioning: review campaigns (annual, professional, 360), continuous feedback, objective tracking and people reviews. Clean interface, HR teams like it, deployment goes smoothly. So far so good.

The real frustrations show up after a few months of use:

- **Training is the poor relation**: Elevo lets you set development objectives, but the training plan itself, needs collection, budget tracking — none of that is covered. If you have legal training obligations, that's a problem.
- **No HR admin**: no leave management, no payroll, no employee files. Centralisation means pairing Elevo with another tool.
- **Cost at scale**: past 200 employees, the bill stops being competitive against tools that cover a wider scope for the same price.
- **Mandatory professional reviews**: the legal compliance layer (review every 2 years, 6-year assessment) isn't always handled as rigorously as with specialists like Empowill.
- **Reporting stays light**: dashboards exist, but cross-referencing reviews, training and performance is basic.

If one of those points hits home, the rest of this should help you find the tool that actually fits.

## 1. Empowill: the reference for reviews and training in SMEs

**Our rating: 4.5/5**

Empowill is the most natural Elevo alternative for SMEs that take talent management seriously. Where Elevo focuses on feedback and people reviews, Empowill goes one step further by natively handling **professional training management**: needs collection, training plan, budget tracking, compliance with legal obligations. That's a real differentiator in the French market.

On reviews, Empowill matches Elevo. Mandatory professional reviews (every 2 years) are managed properly: templates compliant with the Labour Code, automatic deadline alerts, automatic 6-year assessment. For HR teams who used to track delays in an Excel spreadsheet, the time saved is immediate.

The tool also covers 360 feedback campaigns, objective tracking, and dashboards that link reviews and training. That global view of employee development is what sets it apart.

| Criterion | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Price** | 4 to 7 euros per employee per month depending on the package |
| **Target** | SMEs of 50 to 500 employees |
| **Training** | Native full training plan |
| **Deployment** | 2 to 3 weeks |
| **Support** | Chat, email, dedicated CSM, HR support |

### Strengths

- Integrated training management (unique in this segment)
- Professional reviews 100% Labour Code-compliant
- 6-year assessment automatically generated
- 360 feedback and people reviews
- Personalised HR support
- Competitive pricing versus Elevo

### Weaknesses

- No HRIS or payroll (talent specialist only)
- Interface less polished than Elevo on a few screens
- Integration ecosystem still growing

**Ideal if:** you manage both reviews and training, you need to be compliant on mandatory professional reviews, and you want a tool that goes further than Elevo on skill development.

## 2. Javelo: continuous feedback and OKRs at the centre

**Our rating: 4.1/5**

Javelo is Elevo's most direct competitor. Same DNA: digitise review campaigns, push continuous feedback, structure performance management. On paper the scope is almost identical.

What makes Javelo different is **price and how fast you can deploy**. From 3 euros per employee per month, it's one of the most accessible solutions on the market. Setup is quick, HR teams stay autonomous on campaign creation, and managers adopt it easily thanks to a clean interface.

The flip side: like Elevo, Javelo doesn't cover training or HR admin. If you want to expand the scope, look elsewhere. But if reviews and performance are your core need at a better rate, Javelo ticks the boxes.

## 3. Zest: employee engagement as the compass

Zest comes at the problem from a different angle. Where Elevo starts from formal reviews, Zest starts from **daily engagement**. Daily mood, pulse surveys, social barometers, idea box — the tool measures employee sentiment continuously and gives managers weak signals before they turn into problems.

Zest also covers reviews and objectives, but engagement is the real centre of gravity. If your CEO regularly asks "how are the teams?" and you don't have data to answer, Zest plugs that hole. The dashboards are visual, exportable, and let you track over time by team, site or department.

The other side: professional reviews and training are less structured than at Empowill. And the price climbs quickly with extra modules.

## Migrating from Elevo: the 5 key steps

Switching talent management tool is way less painful than a payroll migration. Still deserves some prep.

### 1. Export your review data

Pull the history of completed reviews, objectives in progress and recorded feedback. Most tools allow CSV or PDF export. Keep these archives — they have legal value for professional reviews.

### 2. Map your current campaigns

If a review campaign is in progress, wait until it ends before migrating. Migrating mid-campaign is the fastest way to lose data and frustrate managers. Plan the migration between two campaigns.

### 3. Pick the right timing

The best window is September, just before end-of-year annual reviews kick off. You get time to configure the new tool, train HR and managers, and launch the campaign on the new system. Avoid November-January, way too busy.

### 4. Configure your templates

Use the migration to clean up your review templates. It's the right moment to simplify, drop unnecessary questions, and align the evaluation grids with your real performance criteria. A shorter review is a more completed review.

### 5. Train managers and communicate

The number one failure factor for any talent management tool is manager adoption. Plan a 30-minute session to walk them through it, send a quick guide. A manager who understands the tool will use it. A manager who doesn't will do their reviews on a napkin.
