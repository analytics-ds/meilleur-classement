## Why look for an Elevo alternative?

Elevo has carved out a place in the talent management tool landscape in France. Its positioning is clear: review campaigns (annual, professional, 360), continuous feedback, objective tracking and people reviews. The interface is clean, HR teams appreciate it, and deployment is relatively frictionless.

However, after a few months of use, some HR teams start hitting concrete limitations:

- **Training remains a poor relation**: Elevo allows you to set development objectives but does not manage the training plan, needs collection or budget tracking. For companies subject to legal training obligations, this is a problem.
- **Lack of HR administrative management**: no leave, no payroll, no employee files. If you want to centralise, you will have to pair Elevo with another tool.
- **Cost at scale**: for organisations over 200 employees, the Elevo bill can become significant compared to tools that cover a wider scope for an equivalent price.
- **Mandatory professional reviews**: legal compliance (review every 2 years, 6-year assessment) is not always managed as rigorously as with specialists like Empowill.
- **Limited reporting**: dashboards exist, but cross-referencing data between reviews, training and performance remains basic.

If one of these points speaks to you, the rest should help you find the tool that best fits your needs.

## 1. Empowill: the benchmark for reviews and training for SMEs

**Our rating: 4.5/5**

Empowill is the most natural alternative to Elevo for SMEs that take talent management seriously. Where Elevo focuses on feedback and people reviews, Empowill goes further by natively integrating **professional training management**: needs collection, training plan, budget tracking and compliance with legal obligations. This is a real differentiator in the French market.

On reviews, Empowill is at least as solid as Elevo. Mandatory professional reviews (every 2 years) are managed rigorously: templates compliant with the Labour Code, automatic deadline alerts, automatic generation of the 6-year assessment. For HR teams who spent hours tracking delays in an Excel spreadsheet, the time saved is immediate.

The tool also offers 360 feedback campaigns, objective tracking and dashboards that cross-reference reviews and training. It is this global view of employee development that makes the difference.

| Criterion | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Price** | 4 to 7 euros per employee per month depending on the package |
| **Target** | SMEs of 50 to 500 employees |
| **Training** | Native full training plan |
| **Deployment** | 2 to 3 weeks |
| **Support** | Chat, email, dedicated CSM, HR support |

### Strengths

- Integrated training management (unique in this segment)
- Professional reviews 100% compliant with the Labour Code
- 6-year assessment automatically generated
- 360 feedback and people reviews
- Personalised HR support
- Competitive pricing versus Elevo

### Weaknesses

- No HRIS or payroll (talent specialist only)
- Interface less "designed" than Elevo on some screens
- Growing integrations ecosystem

**Ideal if:** you manage both reviews and training, you need to be compliant on mandatory professional reviews, and you are looking for a more complete tool than Elevo on skill development.

## 2. Javelo: continuous feedback and OKRs at the heart of the system

**Our rating: 4.1/5**

Javelo is Elevo's most direct competitor. The two tools share a common DNA: digitising review campaigns, encouraging continuous feedback, and structuring performance management. On paper, the scope is almost identical.

Where Javelo stands out is on **price and deployment simplicity**. With a rate starting at 3 euros per employee per month, it is one of the most accessible solutions on the market. Configuration is quick, HR teams are autonomous on campaign creation, and adoption by managers is generally good thanks to a clean interface.

On the other hand, like Elevo, Javelo does not cover training or HR administration. If you are looking to expand the scope, it is not the right choice. But if your main need remains performance and reviews at a better rate, Javelo ticks the boxes.

Zest takes the problem from a different angle. Where Elevo starts from formal reviews, Zest starts from **daily engagement**. Daily mood, pulse surveys, social barometers, idea box: the tool continuously measures employee sentiment and gives managers weak signals before they become problems.

Zest also covers reviews and objectives, but engagement is clearly at the centre. If your general management regularly asks "how are the teams?" and you do not have data to answer, Zest fills that gap. Engagement dashboards are visual, exportable, and allow tracking over time by team, site or department.

The flip side: professional reviews and training are less structured than at Empowill. And the price can rise quickly with additional modules.

- How much are you currently paying with Elevo, and for how many employees?

## Migration from Elevo: the 5 key steps

Changing talent management tool is less heavy than a payroll migration, but it deserves some preparation.

### 1. Export your review data

Retrieve the history of reviews carried out, objectives in progress and recorded feedback. Most tools allow CSV or PDF export. Keep these archives: they have legal value for professional reviews.

### 2. Map your current campaigns

If a review campaign is in progress, wait for it to finish before migrating. Migrating in the middle of a campaign is the best way to lose data and frustrate managers. Plan the migration between two campaigns.

### 3. Choose the right timing

The best time is September, just before the launch of end-of-year annual reviews. This gives you time to configure the new tool, train HR and managers, and launch the campaign on the new system. Avoid the November-January period, which is too busy.

### 4. Configure the templates in the new tool

Take advantage of the migration to review your review templates. It is often the right time to simplify, remove unnecessary questions and align evaluation grids with your real performance criteria. A shorter review is a better-completed review.

### 5. Train managers and communicate

The number one failure factor for a talent management tool is manager adoption. Plan a 30-minute session to show them the new tool, and send a quick guide. A manager who understands the tool will use it. A manager who does not understand it will do their reviews on the back of a napkin.
