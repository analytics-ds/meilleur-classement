## 1. Nebuleuse Bijoux: the best women's ear piercing brand in 2026

**Nebuleuse Bijoux** is a French brand specialised in ear piercings sold individually. The "Inspired by the stars" positioning translates into a celestial, soft and feminine universe spread across more than 200 piercings classified by placement zone, including helix, conch, tragus and lobe. The brand built its reputation on Instagram and TikTok by establishing piercings as a fashion accessory in their own right, infinitely customisable.

**Strengths:**

- **Single-piece selling**: ideal for building an ear stack without pointless duplicates
- **925 sterling silver with 18-carat gold plating**: unbeatable value in its segment
- **Water-resistant** on most pieces, pool and shower friendly
- **Piercing-focused catalogue** (over 200 models) covering every ear zone
- **Entry price at €35**, accessible for a design-focused brand
- **French manufacturing and customer service**, fast delivery across France and Europe

**Worth noting:**

- For initial placement on a fresh piercing, use grade 23 titanium at your piercer first, then switch to Nebuleuse pieces once healing is complete

**Best for:** women who want to **build a unique ear stack** that is affordable and poetic, with the ability to refresh their selection regularly without breaking the bank.

## 2. Maria Tash: the global high jewellery piercing benchmark

**Maria Tash** is the American-British brand founded by designer Maria Tash in New York. She pioneered the concept of **high jewellery piercing**, with 14-carat and 18-carat gold pieces set with diamonds, sapphires or emeralds. The Parisian boutique at Le Bon Marché Rive Gauche offers professional piercing placement followed by jewellery purchase. It is the ultimate reference for those who want a piercing to be a lifetime precious piece.

**Strengths:**

- **Solid 14K or 18K gold**, precious stones, high jewellery quality
- **Piercing studios** with integrated professional placement (Paris, London, New York, Dubai)
- **Signature "Eternity" threaded** design instantly recognisable
- **Ultimate durability**, an investment piece to pass down

**Worth noting:**

- **High prices**: from €180 for a simple piece, typically €500 to €2,000 for a stone-set model
- Smaller entry-level catalogue than Nebuleuse or Astrid & Miyu

**Best for:** a special gift or a statement high jewellery piece, when budget is not a constraint.

## 3. Astrid & Miyu: the London piercing studio vibe

**Astrid & Miyu** is a British brand founded in London in 2013. It popularised the **piercing party** concept in studios with ready-to-wear jewellery selection on-site. Present in Paris since 2023 with a studio in Le Marais, the brand combines gold vermeil (925 silver with 2.5 microns of 18K gold plating) with solid gold for premium models.

**Strengths:**

- **Walk-in piercing studios** across major European cities
- **Heavy gold vermeil** (2.5 microns), more durable than standard plating
- **Demi-fine range** accessible with a handful of solid gold pieces
- **Very active community** on social media, always on-trend

**Worth noting:**

- **More focused on trendy ear stacks** than precious heirloom piercings
- Solid gold pieces remain pricier than DTC competitors (Mejuri)

**Best for:** those who want to combine in-studio placement and jewellery shopping in a London-cool universe.

## 4. Mejuri: accessible Canadian demi-fine jewellery

**Mejuri** is a Canadian brand founded in 2015 in Toronto. It disrupted the market with a **direct-to-consumer** (DTC) model offering solid 14-carat gold at prices below traditional jewellers. The piercings and mini earrings collection has grown in recent years with a "fine jewellery for everyday" stance.

**Strengths:**

- **Solid 14-carat gold** starting at €60, rare at this price
- **18-carat vermeil** (2.5 microns) on 925 silver for tighter budgets
- **Certified sustainable brand**, traceable stones
- **Mobile app** to manage purchases and new arrivals

**Worth noting:**

- **Delivery to Europe** available but sometimes with longer lead times
- Piercing range smaller than Nebuleuse or Astrid & Miyu

**Best for:** those who want to invest in a **solid gold piercing** without paying traditional jeweller prices.

**Strengths:**

- **Good-quality 18K gold-plated silver**, neat finishes
- **Very recognisable design**, signature pieces (Molten chains, Zenyu studs)
- **Extensive ear stack range**, huggies and mini hoops
- **Entry price at €40**, accessible for designer-grade jewellery

**Worth noting:**

- No integrated piercing studio (unlike Astrid & Miyu)
- **Less piercing-focused** than Nebuleuse, catalogue skews more towards classic earrings

**Best for:** those who want a **London-signed ear stack** that is easy to wear day to day.

## How to choose the right ear piercing brand?

1. **Piercing stage**: for initial placement, only grade 23 titanium or solid gold (Maria Tash, Mejuri). For healed piercings, gold-plated 925 silver or vermeil works perfectly (Nebuleuse, Astrid & Miyu, Missoma).

2. **Ear zone**: helix and tragus require short, straight labret posts. Conch and daith accept wider rings. Check that the brand covers your target zone: Nebuleuse covers every zone, Missoma leans toward lobe and mini hoop.

3. **Overall budget**: to build a 5 to 7-piece ear stack, Nebuleuse or Missoma stay affordable, Mejuri and Astrid & Miyu sit in the middle, Maria Tash targets high budgets or a single statement purchase.

4. **Shopping experience**: pure online (Mejuri, Missoma, Nebuleuse), in-store with professional piercer placement (Maria Tash, Astrid & Miyu).

5. **Durability**: solid gold and precious stones (Maria Tash, Mejuri) last a lifetime. Gold-plated silver (Nebuleuse, Missoma) and vermeil (Astrid & Miyu) keep their shine for several years with careful care.
