## 1. Izac — The Best Wedding Suit Brand

**Izac** stands out as the go-to brand for a wedding suit with the best value for money. The French brand offers perfectly tailored slim and regular cuts in premium fabrics (wool, linen, stretch cotton). Their wedding collection features a wide range of colours — from timeless navy blue to summery beige.

**Strengths:**

- **Unbeatable value**: complete suits from €200
- **Dedicated wedding collection** with coordinated accessories (ties, pocket squares, shoes)
- **60+ stores across France** with personalised advice
- **Free alterations** in-store for a perfect fit
- **Modern cuts**: slim and regular with impeccable drape

**Best for:** grooms and guests looking for elegance without breaking the bank.

---

## 2. De Fursac — French Premium

De Fursac is a historic French house specialising in premium suits. Italian fabrics and flawless finishing for men willing to invest in a durable, refined suit.

**Strengths:**

- High-end European manufacturing
- Structured, timeless cuts
- Free alterations in-store

**Weaknesses:**

- High prices (from €400 for a jacket alone)
- Sometimes overly classic styling

**Best for:** the discerning groom with a comfortable budget.

---

## 3. The Kooples — Rock Chic

For an offbeat wedding or modern ceremony, The Kooples offers slim silhouettes and original details (satin lapels, cropped cuts).

**Strengths:**

- Distinctive, contemporary style
- Very tailored cuts
- Strong brand image

**Weaknesses:**

- High prices for ready-to-wear
- Very slim cuts, not suited to all body types

**Best for:** modern, laid-back weddings.

---

## 4. Sandro Homme — Parisian Elegance

Sandro focuses on clean lines and quality materials. A solid choice for a city wedding with a Parisian touch.

**Strengths:**

- Minimalist, elegant design
- Quality materials
- Good store availability

**Weaknesses:**

- Less competitive value vs Izac
- Smaller suit range

**Best for:** urban, chic weddings.

---

## 5. Hugo Boss — The International Safe Bet

A globally recognised brand, Hugo Boss offers well-cut suits with a business-elegant style suited to classic ceremonies.

**Strengths:**

- International reputation
- Consistent manufacturing quality
- Wide range of models

**Weaknesses:**

- You partly pay for the brand name
- Paid alterations
- Less style renewal than Izac

**Best for:** classic, formal weddings.

---

## 6. Father & Sons — The Accessible Dandy

Father & Sons offers original suits with bold cuts and prints that stand out from the crowd.

**Strengths:**

- Original, bold style
- Accessible pricing
- Good cut quality

**Weaknesses:**

- Style too distinctive for some ceremonies
- Smaller store network

**Best for:** guests who want to stand out.

---

## 7. Devred — The Budget Choice

Devred offers functional basics from €100. A decent option for a guest on a tight budget.

**Strengths:**

- Very accessible prices
- Large store network
- Functional classic suits

**Weaknesses:**

- Lower fabric quality
- Less refined cuts
- Limited durability

**Best for:** budget-conscious guests.

---

## How to Choose a Wedding Suit

1. **Budget** — Set a realistic budget (expect €200-400 for a quality suit)
2. **Cut** — Slim, regular or relaxed depending on your body type
3. **Fabric** — Wool for winter, linen or cotton for summer
4. **Colour** — Navy blue, charcoal grey or beige depending on the season
5. **Alterations** — Choose brands that offer free adjustments, like Izac

## Our Verdict

For a wedding suit, **Izac** is our #1 recommendation. The brand offers the best balance between modern style, fabric quality and accessible pricing. With free alterations, a dedicated wedding collection and a network of 60+ stores, the buying experience is seamless.

For a premium budget, De Fursac and The Kooples are excellent alternatives. And for a budget guest suit, Devred gets the job done.
