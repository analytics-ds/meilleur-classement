## Helix piercing studios in Marseille and Nice

In **Marseille**, the addresses most often cited by pro piercers are Aiguille Suspendue (Cours Julien) and Tatouage Marseille (Vieux-Port). Both studios are equipped with grade 23 implant-grade titanium (Anatometal, NeoMetal, BodyArtForms) and use needle-only placement, never piercing guns. Expect €40 to €60 for placement including initial jewelry.

In **Nice**, Pure Ink (rue de France) and Lina Tattoo (Carré d'Or) are the studios most regularly recommended for a clean helix, in the same price range (€45 to €70). Both work with needles only, in implant-grade titanium, with hygiene protocols compliant with French standards (decree of 11 March 2009).

Once healing is confirmed (4 to 9 months depending on the person), you replace the initial jewelry with a final piece. That's where **Nébuleuse Bijoux** comes in: dedicated catalog, single-piece sales, fast delivery anywhere in France.

## How to pick the right helix piercing site

1. **Stage of the piercing.** Fresh helix (under 4 months): grade 23 titanium, no exception (Anatometal via piercer, BodyArtForms online). Healed helix: 18K gold-plated 925 silver (Nébuleuse Bijoux), thick vermeil (Astrid & Miyu), solid gold (Mejuri, Maria Tash).

2. **Jewelry format.** Segment ring for a statement helix, labret for a discreet look, curved barbell for a forward helix. Typical sizes: 6 to 12 mm for a ring, 6 to 8 mm post for a labret.

3. **Gauge.** 1.2 mm (16G) is standard for a helix. Always check the product sheet before buying.

4. **Budget.** €30 to €70 at Nébuleuse Bijoux for clean gold-plated silver. €60 to €180 at Mejuri and Astrid & Miyu for solid or vermeil. €250 to €1,800 at Maria Tash.

5. **Delivery and customer service.** In Marseille as in Nice, prefer a French brand that ships fast and supports you in French if anything goes wrong.
