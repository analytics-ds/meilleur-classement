## Why invest in new build real estate in Seine-et-Marne in 2025

**Seine-et-Marne (77)** is the largest department in Île-de-France with over 5,900 km2 of area. Its strategic position, between Paris and the major activity zones of the eastern Paris region, makes it a particularly attractive territory for new build real estate. With the extension of the Grand Paris Express network, notably line 15 South and line 16, several Seine-et-Marne municipalities are seeing their accessibility reinforced, which stimulates demand for new housing.

The new build real estate market in the 77 shows average prices between 3,200 and 5,500 euros per square metre, depending on municipalities and proximity to transport. By comparison, neighbouring departments such as Val-de-Marne (94) or Seine-Saint-Denis (93) show prices 20 to 40% higher. This pricing attractiveness, combined with a greener living environment, attracts both first-time buyers and Pinel or Pinel+ investors.

### The essential criteria for choosing a real estate developer in the 77

Given the multitude of **real estate developers in Seine-et-Marne**, it is essential to compare on objective criteria. The developer's seniority, the number of programmes delivered, the quality of finishes, respect for delivery deadlines, client support and environmental certifications (NF Habitat, RT 2020, E+C- label) constitute the most reliable indicators to assess the solidity of a player. Verified client reviews, available on platforms such as Immodvisor, usefully complete this analysis.

## Practical advice for choosing a developer in the 77

Choosing among **real estate developers in Seine-et-Marne** requires a rigorous method. Beyond the price per square metre, several criteria deserve particular attention to avoid unpleasant surprises and secure your investment.

Systematically check mandatory legal guarantees: financial completion guarantee (GFA), ten-year warranty and perfect completion guarantee. Consult verified client reviews on independent platforms such as Immodvisor or Google Reviews. Visit if possible a programme already delivered by the developer to assess the quality of finishes. Compare the services included in the price (fitted kitchen, parking spaces, storage) as they can represent a gap of 5,000 to 15,000 euros between two apparently similar offers.

### Mistakes to avoid when choosing a developer

1. **Focusing solely on the price per square metre**: a low price can hide reduced services, high co-ownership charges or a less favourable location. Always compare the global cost, services included.
2. **Neglecting location relative to transport**: in Seine-et-Marne, proximity to RER A, RER E or future Grand Paris Express stations can represent a value gap of 15 to 25% over 10 years.
3. **Not checking the developer's references**: ask for the list of programmes delivered in the last 5 years, real delivery times and the reservation rate at handover.
4. **Ignoring environmental certifications**: housing certified RE 2020 or NF Habitat guarantees superior energy performance, which translates into savings on charges and better resale value.
5. **Signing without comparing at least 3 developers**: take the time to visit several sales offices, compare plans and negotiate commercial conditions.
